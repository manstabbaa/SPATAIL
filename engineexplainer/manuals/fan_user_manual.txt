AXICOOL 60 — AXIAL COOLING FAN
User Manual & Quick-Start Guide
Model AC-60X · 60 × 60 × 10 mm · 12 V DC

------------------------------------------------------------
IN THE BOX
------------------------------------------------------------
- 1 × AxiCool 60 axial fan (frame + 7-blade rotor)
- 4 × M3 mounting screws
- 1 × 2-pin power lead

------------------------------------------------------------
KNOW YOUR FAN
------------------------------------------------------------
The fan has two main parts. The square FRAME holds the fan in
place and protects the moving parts. The ROTOR is the spinning
hub with seven curved BLADES that move the air. The blades are
angled like shallow ramps so that, as the rotor turns, they
scoop air and push it through the frame.

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 1 — IDENTIFY THE AIRFLOW DIRECTION
------------------------------------------------------------
Look at the curve of the blades. Air is pulled in on the intake
side and pushed out the exhaust side. Mount the fan so the
exhaust faces the component you want to cool.

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 2 — MOUNT THE FRAME
------------------------------------------------------------
Align the four corner holes of the frame with the mounting
points. Insert an M3 screw into each corner and tighten gently.
Do not overtighten — the frame is plastic and can crack.

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 3 — CONNECT POWER
------------------------------------------------------------
Plug the 2-pin lead into a 12 V DC header. Red is positive,
black is ground. The motor inside the hub drives the rotor.

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 4 — POWER ON AND SPIN UP
------------------------------------------------------------
Apply power. The hub begins to turn, carrying all seven blades
as a single rigid assembly. Within one second the rotor reaches
full speed and a steady stream of air flows through the frame.

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 5 — VERIFY AIRFLOW
------------------------------------------------------------
Hold a slip of paper near the exhaust side. It should flutter
steadily, confirming continuous airflow. The faster the blades
spin, the more air is moved per second.

------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 6 — MAINTENANCE
------------------------------------------------------------
Every few months, power down and let the rotor stop completely.
Gently clear dust from the blades with a soft brush. Never insert
objects into the spinning rotor.

------------------------------------------------------------
SPECIFICATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------
Voltage:        12 V DC
Airflow:        ~12 CFM
Noise:          22 dBA
Bearing:        sleeve
Blade count:    7
